# Pollo / Seedance Video Ideas

Shortlist from live Instagram Reels searches run through the local Reels Scout API.

## Best Demo Angle

Use the scout to find proven short-form ad patterns, then use Pollo / Seedance to create original versions. The point is not to copy a viral Reel. The point is to stop prompting blindly.

Recommended on-camera path:

1. Open Reels Scout.
2. Click `UGC`.
3. Star 2-3 references.
4. Click a card-level `Pollo` button to copy the source URL and open Pollo.
5. Generate original variants: UGC, product promo, review, unboxing, explainer, AI creator.
6. Mention the API only after the manual workflow: scale winners, not random outputs.

## Product Scenario

Use one fictional product so the demo feels concrete:

**Solara SPF 50**, a compact sunscreen stick for creators, commuters, and travel.

Why this works:

- UGC and beauty/skincare Reels are easy to understand visually.
- The product supports close-ups, texture shots, unboxing, review, routine, and outdoor payoff scenes.
- It avoids copying any real sponsor or competitor brand.

Avoid making a Reel that advertises another AI ad platform your main on-screen reference. Those can help you think through the structure, but the cleanest sponsor-safe examples are UGC product shots, generic product promos, and AI-native product commercials.

## Prompts To Try In Pollo / Seedance

### 1. UGC Hook Test

Create a 9:16 UGC-style video ad for a fictional sunscreen stick called Solara SPF 50. Handheld creator footage, casual bathroom counter setup, natural morning light, quick product close-ups, texture swipe on skin, then a confident outdoor payoff shot. Start with a fast hook: "I finally found sunscreen I actually keep in my bag." Make it feel authentic, lightly imperfect, and made for Instagram Reels. No real brand logos, no celebrity likeness, no copied creator identity.

### 2. Product Promo

Create a polished 9:16 product promo for Solara SPF 50 sunscreen stick. Begin with the product rotating on a clean reflective surface, cut to macro texture shots, sunlight flare, water droplets, and a smooth travel-bag placement shot. Visual style: premium skincare ad, warm daylight, clean typography space, crisp product hero frames. End with the product in hand before stepping outside.

### 3. Review Clip

Create a 9:16 review-style creator video for Solara SPF 50. Faceless creator setup, hands only, product on a desk with phone and keys. Show three beats: first impression, texture test, daily-use reason. Make it feel like an honest micro-review, not a glossy commercial. Use natural hand movement, realistic tabletop lighting, and quick cutaways.

### 4. Unboxing

Create a 9:16 unboxing video for Solara SPF 50. Start with a sealed minimal package on a desk, hands opening it, tissue paper reveal, close-up of the product, cap twist, texture swatch, and final shot dropping it into a small bag. Make the pacing satisfying and tactile: soft paper sounds, clean close-ups, warm shadows, premium but realistic.

### 5. Explainer Ad

Create a 9:16 explainer ad for Solara SPF 50. Show a simple visual story: morning routine, commuting, outdoor coffee, reapplying sunscreen from a bag. Use clear visual beats that explain why a sunscreen stick is easier than a messy bottle. Leave clean space for captions like: "No spills", "No white cast", "Reapply anywhere". Modern, bright, practical.

### 6. AI Creator / Floating Product Ad

Create a 9:16 AI-native product ad for Solara SPF 50. A sunscreen stick floats through a stylized summer environment: sunlight, glass water, sand-colored surface, translucent SPF shield effect, product assembling from clean geometric parts, then landing in a creator's hand. Cinematic macro lens, smooth speed ramps, premium skincare-commercial lighting. Keep it original and avoid real brand logos.

### 7. Direct Response Ad

Create a 9:16 direct-response ad for Solara SPF 50. Hook with the problem: messy sunscreen bottles leak in bags. Show the solution: compact sunscreen stick, quick reapply, clean hands, travel-friendly use. Fast cuts, readable caption space, practical product moments, creator-style energy, final CTA frame with a simple product hero shot.
